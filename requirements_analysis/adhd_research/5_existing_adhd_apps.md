# Existing ADHD Apps Analysis

## Popular Apps & What We Can Learn:

### 1. Todoist (Not ADHD-specific, but widely used)
**Strengths**: Simple, fast, cross-platform, clean interface
**Weaknesses**: Doesn't understand ADHD psychology (broken streaks induce guilt, rigid structure)
**What to Copy**: Speed of adding tasks, minimalistic design
**What to Avoid**: Perfection tracking, shame-based notifications

### 2. Brili (Built for ADHD/Routines)
**Strengths**: Excellent visual timer, ADHD-friendly routine builder, positive reinforcement
**Weaknesses**: Expensive subscription, overly rigid for bad ADHD days, iOS only
**What to Copy**: Visual time representation, routine flexibility
**What to Avoid**: High price point, lack of Android support

### 3. Goblin Tools (Built for Neurodivergent)
**Strengths**: Magical task decomposition, understanding of executive dysfunction, free
**Weaknesses**: Web-only, limited features, no mobile app
**What to Copy**: AI-powered task breakdown, empathy in design
**What to Avoid**: Limited platform availability

### 4. Forest (Focus & Motivation)
**Strengths**: Gamification works well for ADHD, visual progress, simple concept
**Weaknesses**: Too simplistic for complex needs, only does one thing (focus timer)
**What to Copy**: Engaging visual feedback, immediate rewards
**What to Avoid**: Single-function limitation

### 5. Tiimo (ADHD & Autism)
**Strengths**: Truly neurodivergent-designed, visual scheduling, flexible routines
**Weaknesses**: Complex setup, expensive, steep learning curve
**What to Copy**: True neurodiversity understanding, visual-first design
**What to Avoid**: Complexity barrier to entry

### 6. Focusmate (Body Doubling)
**Strengths**: Solves ADHD accountability perfectly, human connection
**Weaknesses**: Requires social interaction (anxiety trigger for some), subscription model
**What to Copy**: Accountability through social commitment
**What to Avoid**: Mandatory social component

## Design Patterns That Work for ADHD:

### **Effective Patterns:**
- **Visual over textual** - Icons, colors, timelines
- **Immediate feedback** - Quick rewards for small actions
- **Low barrier to entry** - Start using in under 60 seconds
- **Forgiving design** - Miss a day? No problem, start fresh
- **Customizable notifications** - Gentle, encouraging, never nagging

### **Ineffective Patterns (Common in Competitors):**
- ‚ùå **Complex onboarding** - Lose users before they start
- ‚ùå **Perfection tracking** - Streaks that break and cause abandonment
- ‚ùå **Information overload** - Too many options upfront
- ‚ùå **Rigid systems** - No accommodation for ADHD variability
- ‚ùå **Shame-based messaging** - "You failed your streak!"
